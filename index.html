What is Gender Equality?

Gender equality refers to the right of women and men to have the same opportunities for the achievement of important goals i​n society such as education, employment and income and to contribut​e to political, social, and cultural development at all levels.

read more

Why it matters?

The ​goal of gender equality is to empower all women and and to promote equitable and respectable advancement of men and women together.

Gender equality is a fundamental human right. Advancing gender equality is critical to all areas of a healthy society, from reducing poverty to promoting the health, education, protection and the well-being of girls and boys. 

Challenges

The Leaky Pipeline

Gender Pay Gap

Occupational Segregation

Women tend to face more difficulties to progress in their careers, to be paid less, and to be concentrated in fewer jobs than men. According to studies, there are three key gender equality challenges.

The Leaky Pipeline

Women make up only five percent of CEOs and fourteen percent of executive committees of Fortune 500 companies. Across all industries and countries, the more you climb the corporate ladder, the fewer women you see. 

Women at entry level are three times less likely than men to be promoted to the next level and the gap continues at every transitional point.

Gender Pay Gap

Despite the legislation in developed countries to guarantee equal pay, the gender pay gap persists. On average, globally, women earn 23 percent less than man. Some structural factors explain partially the difference: women tend to occupy less well-remunerated positions, less senior roles, and more part-time roles. However, there remains a gap for which no reasonable explanation can be given.

A data shows that based on 6,971 skilled workers shows that on average, male employees in the Philippines earn ₱ 5,000 or $100 USD more than their female counterparts.

Occupational Segregation

Women and men tend to be segregated into different industries and types of jobs. Women tend to be concentrated in fewer occupations, mostly in clerical, service, and professional occupations. Segregation starts early, with clear distinctions between the degrees chosen by men and by women. “Traditional career paths” offer women less of a choice, and deprive whole sectors of female talents.

Promote Gender Equality

What can we do to fix these issues? Check out what adults can do to advance gender equality.

Share Household Chores

Ask yourself if there is an equal division of labor in your home. The work of caring for children is still done mainly by women in much of the world. A study conducted by the UN in 2015 concluded that women from developing countries do over three hours more of daily unpaid work than men, while in developed countries, they do, on average, two hours more. All adults living in the same home should take responsibility for the household chores.

Hire Diversity

Value, stimulate and hire diverse teams. One way to increase the hiring of women and other underrepresented groups is to remove names, photos and addresses from the resumes early in the selection process because, according to studies, gender and race biases can affect what happens even before the interview.

Sexual Harassment: Zero Tolerance

Workplace harassment is a form of unlawful employment discrimination that creates an creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.  Sexual harassment is a form of workplace harassment that is prohibited and will not be tolerated in any form. This involves unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.

Employees must have a safe channel to report bullying, sexual harassment and racism without being exposed or dismissed. Proven cases should be punished. 

Help Women Gain Power

According to a study conducted in 2017 by UN Women, only 17 countries have female heads of state and/or government. The overall average of women in legislative bodies is only 23.4 percent. Find out who the female candidates are, especially women from underrepresented communities.

In addition to politics, consider ways to support the promotion of women and LGBTQI in the arts, sciences, sports, and other fields.

Listen and Reflect

One of the main obstacles to eliminating prejudice is that people have difficulty recognizing that it really exists. Various research and studies have already proven that we all reproduce stereotypes and pre-existing notions on many topics, including gender and race.

So, try to pay attention to your assumptions and know that none of us is immune to making bigoted comments or holding biased attitudes. When someone points out something problematic in your speech, listen and reflect on it.
